Xplorer is a real-time, bi-directional software editor for the Oberheim Xpander and Matrix-12 analog synthesizers. Every knob turn on your synth is reflected instantly in Xplorer — and every change you make in Xplorer is sent to the synth with minimal latency. No patch reloads. No resynchronization.

## What Xplorer does

Xplorer gives you a complete overview of a single patch — including the full Modulation Matrix — in one screen. You can tweak sound simultaneously with a mouse, a hardware MIDI controller, your DAW, or directly on the synthesizer. All methods stay in sync automatically.

## Get started in three steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download and install">
    Grab the latest release from [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/xplorer2716/XplorerEditor/releases), unzip the archive, and launch **Xplorer.exe**. No installer required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure MIDI">
    Open **Settings → MIDI** and select your synth's MIDI OUT and MIDI IN ports. Optionally add an automation input device for hardware controller support.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start editing">
    Xplorer automatically requests the current patch from your synthesizer on first start. Turn a knob on the synth or click a control in Xplorer — both stay in sync in real time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Xplorer runs on Windows 7, 8, 10, and 11 (x86/x64). A minimum display resolution of 1280×900 is required. Mac users can run Xplorer via virtualization — see [Installation](/installation) for details.
</Note>
