This guide walks you from a freshly installed copy of Xplorer to your first live parameter edit on your synthesizer.

## Prerequisites

- Xplorer is [installed](/installation) and ready to launch.
- Your Oberheim Xpander or Matrix-12 is powered on.
- A MIDI interface is connected to your computer, with a MIDI OUT cable running to the synthesizer's MIDI IN, and a MIDI IN cable running from the synthesizer's MIDI OUT.

<Info>
  If you plan to use Xplorer alongside a DAW, set up a virtual MIDI cable (such as [LoopBe1](http://www.nerds.de/en/loopbe1.html)) before continuing so you can route MIDI between applications without a hardware loop.
</Info>

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch Xplorer">
    Double-click `Xplorer.exe`. A splash screen appears while the application initialises.

    When Xplorer starts for the first time, it automatically requests the current patch from your synthesizer. You will see a greeting message on your synthesizer's VFD display showing the Xplorer version number and the GitHub repository URL — this confirms that MIDI communication is working in both directions.

    <Note>
      If the VFD display does not show the greeting, your MIDI output port is not configured yet. Continue to the next step to set it up.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure your MIDI ports">
    Open **Settings → MIDI Configuration** and set:

    - **Synth MIDI OUT** — the port connected to your synthesizer's MIDI IN.
    - **Synth MIDI IN** — the port connected to your synthesizer's MIDI OUT.
    - **MIDI Channel** — must match the channel configured on your Xpander or Matrix-12.
    - **Synth type** — select **Xpander** or **Matrix-12** to match your hardware.

    Click **OK** to apply. Xplorer restarts the MIDI engine immediately.

    <Warning>
      Sending SysEx to the wrong MIDI channel or device can overwrite patch data on unintended equipment. Double-check your port and channel settings before proceeding.
    </Warning>

    For a detailed walkthrough of every MIDI setting, see the [MIDI Setup guide](/guides/midi-setup).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify bi-directional sync">
    To confirm that communication is working in both directions:

    1. **Synth → Xplorer**: Turn any knob on your synthesizer (for example, VCF Frequency). The corresponding control in Xplorer should move in real time and the parameter name should appear in the status display at the top of the Xplorer window.
    2. **Xplorer → Synth**: Click and drag any control in Xplorer (for example, the VCF Frequency slider). You should hear the sound change on your synthesizer immediately.

    If either direction is not working, revisit your MIDI port assignments in **Settings → MIDI Configuration**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Make your first edit">
    You are now in sync. Every change flows instantly in both directions:

    - **Mouse** — click and drag any knob or slider in Xplorer.
    - **Hardware controller** — if you have assigned MIDI CCs, twist a knob on your controller.
    - **Synthesizer front panel** — turn any knob directly on the Xpander or Matrix-12.
    - **DAW automation** — record or play back CC automation through a virtual MIDI cable.

    The Xplorer status display shows the name of the parameter being edited and its current value in real time, regardless of the input source.

    <Tip>
      Use **Copy / Paste** on any ENV, LFO, TRACK, or RAMP page to duplicate settings between slots (e.g., copy all of ENV1's parameters to ENV3) — a fast way to build complex modulation structures.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save your patch">
    When you are happy with your sound, save it:

    - **Store to synth** — use **Patch → Store** to write the patch into one of the 100 memory slots on the synthesizer.
    - **Save to disk** — use **File → Save** to write a `.syx` file to your computer for archival or sharing.

    <Note>
      "Store to synth" writes to the synthesizer's internal memory. "Save to disk" creates a file on your computer. Do both to keep your patch safe.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens at startup

Every time Xplorer starts and connects successfully:

1. It sends a **program change** followed by a **single patch request** to the synthesizer, pulling the currently active patch into the editor.
2. It displays a greeting on the synthesizer's VFD panel: the Xplorer version on the first line, and `GITHUB.COM/XPLORER2716/XPLOREREDITOR` on the second line.

This means Xplorer always opens in sync with whatever patch is currently loaded on the synthesizer — no manual reload required.
